Preliminary Concepts (Read Once)

A normal neural network (classifier) sees an image and says "this is a cat" вҖ” it DISCRIMINATES between classes. A generative model does the opposite вҖ” given the label "cat", it can CREATE a brand-new image of a cat that has never existed before. Generative models are how AI can paint, write stories, compose music, or invent faces.

1. What is a Generative Model?

A generative model is a machine learning model that learns the underlying probability distribution of the training data, so it can generate new samples that look similar to the training data.

2. Generative vs Discriminative вҖ” quick view

  • Discriminative model learns P(y | x) вҖ” given input, predict label (classifier).
  • Generative model learns P(x) or P(x | y) вҖ” can generate new x values.

3. Key Deep Generative Models you'll study

  • Boltzmann Machine вҖ” energy-based, stochastic recurrent network.
  • Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM) вҖ” simpler, no intra-layer connections.
  • Deep Belief Network (DBN) вҖ” stack of RBMs.
  • Variational Autoencoder (VAE) вҖ” uses encoder-decoder with probabilistic latent space.
  • Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) вҖ” two networks (generator + discriminator) compete.
  • Diffusion models вҖ” modern (Stable Diffusion, DALL-E).

Q1 вҖ” Generative Adversarial Network (GAN): Architecture & Components

How this question appeared in past papers:

(May'23, 6 marks) Differentiate generative and discriminative models in GAN.

(May'23, 6 marks) Do GANs find real or fake images? If yes explain in detail.

(May'24, 6 marks) Explain Discriminator network.

(May'25, 6 marks) Explain in brief GAN with an example.

(Nov'25, 6 marks) What is the role of the discriminator in GAN? Inputs/outputs of discriminator.

Answer
Imagine a forger trying to make fake currency notes, and a police officer trying to spot fake notes. Every time the police catch a fake, the forger learns from it and makes better fakes. Every time the forger fools the police, the police get smarter at spotting fakes. After many rounds, the forger becomes so good that even experts can't tell fakes from real. That's exactly how a GAN works вҖ” the GENERATOR is the forger, the DISCRIMINATOR is the police officer.

1. Definition

A Generative Adversarial Network (GAN), introduced by Ian Goodfellow in 2014, is a deep learning framework in which TWO neural networks вҖ” a Generator and a Discriminator вҖ” are trained TOGETHER in a competitive game. The generator tries to create fake data that looks real; the discriminator tries to tell real data from fake.

2. Two Components of GAN

(a) Generator (G)

  • Takes random noise z (sampled from a distribution like Gaussian) as input.
  • Outputs a fake sample (e.g., a fake image) G(z).
  • Goal: produce fake samples that look so real that the discriminator can't tell.

(b) Discriminator (D)

  • Takes a sample (real or fake) as input.
  • Outputs a probability D(x) вҲҲ [0, 1] that the sample is REAL.
  • Goal: correctly classify REAL samples as real (D(x) вҶ’ 1) and FAKE samples as fake (D(G(z)) вҶ’ 0).

4. Adversarial training process

  1. Sample random noise z (e.g., Gaussian noise vector of size 100).
  2. Generator produces fake sample G(z).
  3. Take a real sample x from training data.
  4. Discriminator sees both real x and fake G(z); predicts D(x) and D(G(z)).
  5. Compute the loss:
    • Discriminator wants to maximize log D(x) + log(1 вҲ’ D(G(z))) вҶ’ catch fakes.
    • Generator wants to minimize log(1 вҲ’ D(G(z))) вҶ’ fool discriminator.
  6. Update D's weights (one step), then G's weights (one step).
  7. Repeat for many iterations.
  8. Training converges when discriminator can't distinguish real from fake вҶ’ D(x) вүҲ 0.5 for everything.

5. Loss function (minimax game)

minG maxD V(D, G) = Ex~p_data[log D(x)] + Ez~p_z[log(1 вҲ’ D(G(z)))]

6. Inputs and Outputs of Discriminator (detail)

  • Input: a data sample x (image, audio, etc.). Can be real (from training data) or fake (from generator).
  • Output: a single scalar D(x) вҲҲ [0, 1] representing the probability that the input is real.
  • Architecture: usually a standard CNN (for images) ending in a sigmoid neuron.
  • Trained as a binary classifier: real = 1, fake = 0.

7. Example of GAN in action

  • StyleGAN generates extremely realistic fake human faces (see thispersondoesnotexist.com).
  • CycleGAN converts horse photos into zebra photos.
  • Pix2Pix turns sketches into realistic colour images.
  • DCGAN was an early breakthrough for image generation.

8. Challenges in GAN training

  • Mode collapse: generator produces only a few types of samples (e.g., always the same face).
  • Training instability: oscillation between generator and discriminator.
  • Vanishing gradients: if discriminator becomes too good, generator gets no signal.
  • Hard to evaluate: no clear single metric for "quality".

Key points to remember

  • GAN = 2 networks (Generator + Discriminator) competing in a min-max game.
  • Generator G(z) takes noise вҶ’ produces fake samples.
  • Discriminator D(x) takes a sample вҶ’ outputs probability it is real.
  • Generator tries to fool D; D tries to catch fakes.
  • Discriminator input = real or fake sample; output = 0 (fake) or 1 (real).
  • Trained iteratively until D can no longer distinguish real from fake.
  • Challenges: mode collapse, instability, vanishing gradients.

Q2 вҖ” Boltzmann Machine

Asked 6 times(May'23, Nov'23, May'24 Г—2, May'25, Nov'25 вҖ” usually 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(Nov'23, 6 marks) Draw and explain architecture of Boltzmann machine.

(Nov'25, 6 marks) What is a Boltzmann Machine? Describe its structure and components.

Answer
A Boltzmann Machine is like a fully-connected room of people, where everyone can talk to everyone else. Each person decides whether to "speak up" (be ON) or "stay quiet" (be OFF) probabilistically, based on what the OTHER people in the room are saying. After many rounds of chatting, the room settles into stable conversational patterns. The "stable patterns" represent what the model has learned.

1. Definition

A Boltzmann Machine is a type of stochastic recurrent neural network and a generative energy-based model. It consists of binary units (neurons) that are fully connected to each other (visible-visible, hidden-hidden, visible-hidden). It learns by adjusting the weights so that the probability of training data is maximized.

2. Structure and components

  • Units (neurons): each unit is binary, can be either 0 or 1.
  • Visible units: represent the input data we can observe.
  • Hidden units: capture internal patterns / features of the data.
  • Symmetric weighted connections: every pair of units is connected with a real-valued weight wij (no self-loops).
  • Biases: each unit has its own bias.
  • Energy: the network defines an "energy" for every possible state of all units; low-energy states are most probable.

3. Energy and probability

P(v, h) = eвҲ’E(v, h) / Z (where Z is a normalization constant called the partition function).

5. Objectives of Boltzmann Machine

  • Learn the underlying distribution of the input data.
  • Generate new samples similar to training data.
  • Fill in missing or noisy values.
  • Discover hidden features / latent representations.
  • Solve combinatorial optimization problems (energy-based).

6. Types of Boltzmann Machines

(a) General (Fully-Connected) Boltzmann Machine

  • All units (visible + hidden) connected to all others.
  • Very flexible but training is EXTREMELY slow (intractable).

(b) Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM)

  • Visible units connected ONLY to hidden units (no visible-visible or hidden-hidden connections).
  • Much easier to train using Contrastive Divergence (CD-k).
  • Most popular variant; used as building blocks of Deep Belief Networks.

(c) Deep Boltzmann Machine (DBM)

  • Multiple layers of hidden units; bidirectional connections between layers.
  • More expressive but harder to train than RBM.

(d) Conditional / Convolutional Boltzmann Machines

  • Variants for specific tasks (e.g., images).

7. Training (basics)

  • Use Gibbs sampling to draw samples from the model.
  • Adjust weights to reduce energy of training data and increase energy of generated samples.
  • For RBM, use Contrastive Divergence (CD-k) algorithm вҖ” much faster.

8. Applications

  • Dimensionality reduction.
  • Collaborative filtering (recommendation systems).
  • Pre-training deep neural networks (historical).
  • Feature learning.
  • Anomaly detection.

Key points to remember

  • Boltzmann Machine = stochastic, energy-based, fully-connected generative model.
  • Has visible + hidden binary units with symmetric weights.
  • Types: General BM, Restricted BM (RBM), Deep BM (DBM).
  • Trained using Gibbs sampling / Contrastive Divergence.
  • RBM = most popular; used to build Deep Belief Networks.

Q3 вҖ” Deep Belief Networks (DBN)

Asked 6 times(May'23, Nov'23, May'24, May'25, Nov'25 Г—2 вҖ” usually 5вҖ“6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

Answer

1. Definition

A Deep Belief Network (DBN) is a deep generative model formed by STACKING multiple Restricted Boltzmann Machines (RBMs). The output of one RBM serves as the input to the next, building up a hierarchy of features.

2. Architecture

  • Composed of multiple hidden layers stacked on top of an input (visible) layer.
  • The TOP two layers form a Restricted Boltzmann Machine (bidirectional / undirected connections).
  • The lower layers have DIRECTED (top-down) connections (like a sigmoid belief network).

4. Training process вҖ” Layer-by-layer (greedy) pretraining

  1. Train RBM-1 between visible layer v and first hidden layer hвӮҒ (unsupervised, using contrastive divergence).
  2. Freeze RBM-1. Use hвӮҒ as visible input for RBM-2; train RBM-2 between hвӮҒ and hвӮӮ.
  3. Repeat for each subsequent layer.
  4. (Optional) After pretraining all layers, FINE-TUNE the whole network with supervised learning (backpropagation) for classification tasks.

5. Two phases of DBN

(a) Generative phase

  • The DBN generates new samples that look like the training data.
  • Sample from the top RBM (Gibbs sampling) вҶ’ propagate downward through directed connections вҶ’ produces a new sample at the visible layer.

(b) Discriminative phase

  • After pretraining, add a classification layer on top and fine-tune with labels.
  • The network now ACTS as a classifier вҖ” propagates input UPWARD through the layers and outputs class probabilities.

7. Advantages of DBN

  • Effective unsupervised pretraining for deep networks.
  • Layer-by-layer training avoids vanishing gradients.
  • Learns hierarchical features automatically.
  • Can use both labeled and unlabeled data.

8. Limitations of DBN

  • Training is slow (multiple RBM pretraining + fine-tuning).
  • Largely replaced by modern architectures (CNNs, transformers).
  • Mainly of historical importance now.

9. Applications

  • Handwritten digit recognition (MNIST).
  • Speech recognition.
  • Drug discovery.
  • Collaborative filtering.
  • Pre-training deep nets when labeled data is scarce.

Key points to remember

  • DBN = stacked RBMs trained layer by layer.
  • Top layer = undirected RBM; lower layers = directed.
  • Two phases: generative (top-down sampling) and discriminative (bottom-up classification).
  • Training: unsupervised pretraining then supervised fine-tuning.
  • Historical importance; mostly replaced by modern deep networks.

Q4 вҖ” Applications of GAN

Asked 4 times(May'23, Nov'23, May'24, Nov'25 вҖ” usually 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(May'23, 6 marks) What are applications of GAN? Explain any four in detail.

(Nov'23, 6 marks) List the applications of GAN network with description.

(May'24, 6 marks) Enlist and Explain applications of GAN.

(Nov'25, 6 marks) List at least five real-world applications of GAN and describe any one in detail.

Answer

Top applications of GAN

(a) Image generation

  • Generate ultra-realistic human faces (StyleGAN, ProGAN).
  • Used by websites like "This Person Does Not Exist".
  • Used by artists for new visual content.

(b) Image-to-image translation

  • Convert sketches вҶ’ real photos (Pix2Pix).
  • Convert horses вҶ’ zebras, day вҶ’ night, photo вҶ’ painting (CycleGAN).
  • Used in design, art, gaming.

(c) Super-resolution (SRGAN)

  • Convert low-resolution images into high-resolution ones.
  • Used in surveillance, medical imaging, satellite imagery.

(d) Data augmentation

  • Generate synthetic training examples when real data is scarce.
  • Especially useful in medical imaging (privacy concerns, rare diseases).

(e) Text-to-image generation

  • Generate images from text descriptions (e.g., "a cat riding a bicycle").
  • Used in advertising, content creation, prototyping (StackGAN, AttnGAN).

(f) Deepfake generation

  • Swap faces in videos.
  • Synthesize voices and speech.
  • Used in films (CGI) and unfortunately also in malicious deepfakes.

(g) Style transfer

  • Apply artistic style (Van Gogh, Picasso) to ordinary photos.

(h) 3D object generation

  • Generate 3D models from 2D images.
  • Used in gaming and AR/VR.

(i) Music and audio generation

  • Compose new music, generate speech / sound effects.

(j) Anomaly detection

  • Train GAN on normal data; anomalies are detected when they cannot be well reconstructed.
  • Used in fraud detection, manufacturing defect detection.

(k) Drug discovery

  • Generate new candidate molecular structures.

(l) Video generation and prediction

  • Generate realistic video sequences or predict future frames.

Key points to remember

  • Top GAN applications: image generation, image-to-image translation, super-resolution, data augmentation, text-to-image, deepfakes.
  • Other uses: style transfer, 3D models, music, anomaly detection, drug discovery.
  • Anywhere we need to CREATE new data that looks real, GAN is useful.

Q5 вҖ” Deep Generative Models (overview)

Asked 4 times(May'23, Nov'23, May'25, Nov'25 вҖ” usually 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(Nov'23, 6 marks) Explain Deep generative model with example.

(May'25, 6 marks) Explain Deep Generative Model.

(Nov'25, 6 marks) Explain: Deep Generative Model.

Answer

1. Definition

A Deep Generative Model is a deep neural network that learns the probability distribution of training data and can generate NEW samples that look similar to the training data. It uses many layers (= "deep") to learn complex high-dimensional distributions.

2. Why "deep"?

  • Deep networks can capture complex hierarchical patterns in data (edges вҶ’ textures вҶ’ objects).
  • Simple generative models (Gaussian mixtures) cannot model complex real-world data like images.
  • Deep generative models can generate realistic images, audio, text.

3. Main types of Deep Generative Models

  • Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM) вҖ” energy-based.
  • Deep Belief Network (DBN) вҖ” stacked RBMs.
  • Deep Boltzmann Machine (DBM) вҖ” multi-layer Boltzmann.
  • Variational Autoencoder (VAE) вҖ” probabilistic encoder-decoder.
  • Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) вҖ” generator vs discriminator.
  • Autoregressive models (PixelRNN, PixelCNN) вҖ” predict next pixel given previous.
  • Normalizing Flow models вҖ” exact likelihood with invertible transformations.
  • Diffusion models вҖ” modern; gradually denoise to generate (Stable Diffusion, DALL-E).

4. Comparison of generative models

ModelStrengthWeakness
RBM / DBNEnergy-based, classicSlow training, hard to scale
VAESmooth latent spaceBlurry outputs
GANSharp, realistic outputsTraining instability, mode collapse
DiffusionState-of-the-art qualitySlow inference
AutoregressiveHigh likelihoodSlow sampling

5. Applications of Deep Generative Models

  • Image generation (faces, art).
  • Text generation (story, dialogue).
  • Music composition.
  • Drug discovery (molecule generation).
  • Data augmentation for training.
  • Anomaly detection.
  • Image inpainting, super-resolution.

Key points to remember

  • Deep generative model = deep network that learns data distribution + generates new samples.
  • Types: RBM, DBN, VAE, GAN, Diffusion, Autoregressive, Normalizing Flows.
  • GAN = realistic outputs; VAE = smoother but blurrier; Diffusion = current state-of-the-art.

Q6 вҖ” Types of GAN

Asked 3 times(Nov'23, May'24, May'25 вҖ” usually 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(Nov'23, 6 marks) Explain different types of GAN.

(May'24, 6 marks) State and explain different types of GAN.

(May'25, 6 marks) Explain types of GAN.

Answer

1. Vanilla GAN (original by Goodfellow 2014)

  • The basic GAN with one generator and one discriminator.
  • Uses fully-connected layers; can be unstable.

2. DCGAN (Deep Convolutional GAN)

  • Uses convolutional layers instead of fully connected ones.
  • Much more stable training for image data.
  • Introduced architectural guidelines (batch norm, no pooling, leaky ReLU).

3. Conditional GAN (cGAN)

  • Both generator and discriminator receive an additional CONDITION (label or text).
  • Allows controlled generation: "generate a dog", "generate digit 7".

4. CycleGAN

  • Image-to-image translation WITHOUT paired training data.
  • Uses cycle-consistency loss.
  • Famous for horse вҶ’ zebra, summer вҶ’ winter.

5. Pix2Pix

  • Image-to-image translation WITH paired training data.
  • Used for sketch вҶ’ photo, satellite вҶ’ map, B/W вҶ’ colour.

6. StyleGAN / StyleGAN2 / StyleGAN3

  • State-of-the-art for generating photorealistic faces.
  • Allows fine control over "style" of generated images.

7. SRGAN (Super-Resolution GAN)

  • Converts low-resolution images to high-resolution.

8. ProGAN (Progressive GAN)

  • Trains by progressively growing image resolution from low to high.
  • More stable training for high-res images.

9. WGAN (Wasserstein GAN)

  • Uses Wasserstein distance instead of KL divergence.
  • More stable training, less mode collapse.

10. Other notable types

  • InfoGAN вҖ” disentangles latent space.
  • BigGAN вҖ” generates very high-quality high-resolution images.
  • StackGAN вҖ” text-to-image generation.
  • SeqGAN вҖ” sequence generation (text, music).

11. Summary table

Key points to remember

  • Top types: DCGAN, cGAN, CycleGAN, Pix2Pix, StyleGAN, SRGAN, WGAN.
  • DCGAN = deep convolutional; cGAN = controlled generation.
  • CycleGAN = unpaired translation; Pix2Pix = paired translation.
  • StyleGAN = realistic faces; SRGAN = super-resolution; WGAN = stable training.

Q7 вҖ” Generative vs Discriminative Models in GAN

Asked 1 time(May'23 вҖ” 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(May'23, 6 marks) Differentiate generative and discriminative models in GAN.

Answer

1. Discriminative model

  • Learns to classify data вҖ” given an input x, predict label y.
  • Learns P(y | x) вҖ” conditional probability of label given input.
  • Example: image classifier deciding "cat or dog".
  • In GAN, the DISCRIMINATOR is a discriminative model вҖ” it classifies samples as real or fake.

2. Generative model

  • Learns to generate data вҖ” produce new samples that look like training data.
  • Learns P(x) (or P(x | y) for conditional generation).
  • Example: model that creates new cat images.
  • In GAN, the GENERATOR is a generative model вҖ” it generates fake samples from random noise.

3. Comparison table

AspectDiscriminativeGenerative
LearnsP(y | x)P(x) or P(x | y)
GoalClassify / predict labelGenerate new samples
Role in GANDiscriminator (D)Generator (G)
InputReal or fake sampleRandom noise vector z
OutputProbability sample is realSynthetic sample G(z)
Loss objectiveMaximize accuracy of real vs fakeMaximize "real-ness" of fake
ExamplesSVM, Logistic Regression, CNN classifierVAE, GAN generator, Diffusion model

Key points to remember

  • Discriminative = predict label given input (P(y | x)).
  • Generative = produce new data (P(x)).
  • In GAN: Discriminator = discriminative; Generator = generative.
  • The two work together adversarially: D catches fakes, G fools D.

Q8 вҖ” Discriminator Network in GAN

Asked 2 times(May'24, Nov'25 вҖ” usually 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(May'24, 6 marks) Explain Discriminator network.

(Nov'25, 6 marks) What is the role of the discriminator in GAN? What are the inputs and outputs of a discriminator network?

Answer

1. Role of the Discriminator

  • The discriminator is a binary classifier that tries to distinguish REAL samples (from training data) from FAKE samples (from generator).
  • It is the "police officer" in the GAN forger-vs-police analogy.
  • By trying to catch fakes, it forces the generator to produce more realistic samples over time.

2. Inputs and Outputs

  • Input: a single sample x (image, audio, etc.). Can be either a real sample from training data OR a fake sample produced by the generator.
  • Output: a single scalar D(x) вҲҲ [0, 1] вҖ” the probability that x is REAL.
  • Typically ends in a sigmoid activation that squashes the output to [0, 1].

3. Architecture (for images)

  • Usually a standard CNN (Convolutional Neural Network).
  • Several convolutional layers (often with stride 2 for downsampling).
  • LeakyReLU activations.
  • Batch normalization (often).
  • Final fully-connected layer вҶ’ single sigmoid neuron.

4. Loss function for Discriminator

LD = вҲ’ ( log D(x) + log(1 вҲ’ D(G(z))) )
  • For real samples x: D should output 1 вҶ’ log D(x) should be high.
  • For fake samples G(z): D should output 0 вҶ’ log(1 вҲ’ D(G(z))) should be high.
  • Discriminator wants to MAXIMIZE this objective (minimize the negative).

5. Training procedure for D

  1. Sample a real image x from the training set.
  2. Sample noise z; pass through generator to get fake image G(z).
  3. Compute D(x) and D(G(z)).
  4. Compute loss LD.
  5. Backpropagate and update D's weights only (not G's).

6. Common issues

  • D too strong: if D becomes too good, it gives no useful gradient to G вҶ’ mode collapse / vanishing gradients.
  • D too weak: if D is weak, G never has to improve.
  • Balance is essential вҖ” usually alternate updates of G and D.

Key points to remember

  • Discriminator = binary classifier (real vs fake) in GAN.
  • Input: sample (real or fake). Output: probability sample is real (0вҖ“1).
  • Architecture: usually CNN ending in sigmoid.
  • Trained to maximize log D(x) + log(1 вҲ’ D(G(z))).
  • Must be balanced with generator вҖ” neither too strong nor too weak.

Q9 вҖ” Do GANs Find Real or Fake Images?

Asked 1 time(May'23 вҖ” 6 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(May'23, 6 marks) Do GANs find real or fake images? If yes explain it in detail.

Answer

Yes вҖ” GANs are capable of both generating fake images AND distinguishing real from fake images. These are the two complementary roles of the generator and discriminator inside a GAN.

1. The Generator GENERATES fake images

  • Takes a random noise vector z (e.g., from a Gaussian distribution).
  • Passes it through deep convolutional layers (transposed convolutions, upsampling).
  • Output: a fake image that mimics the real-data distribution.
  • After enough training, the fakes become indistinguishable from real images.

2. The Discriminator DETECTS real vs fake

  • Takes an image x as input (either real from dataset or fake from generator).
  • Passes it through CNN layers.
  • Outputs a probability D(x) in [0, 1].
  • 1 = "this is definitely real"; 0 = "this is definitely fake"; 0.5 = "unsure".

3. The training game

  1. Generator tries to make fakes that fool the discriminator.
  2. Discriminator tries to catch the fakes.
  3. Over time, both improve вҖ” eventually generator produces samples so realistic that even the (trained) discriminator can only guess (D вүҲ 0.5).

4. Use cases

  • Generation: create new artwork, faces, training data (used by artists, designers).
  • Detection: the discriminator alone can be used to detect deepfakes, AI-generated content.
  • Anomaly detection: if discriminator scores a sample very low, it might be an anomaly.

5. Real-world application вҖ” deepfake detection

  • Train a discriminator on real videos and known deepfakes.
  • Use this trained discriminator as a stand-alone fake-image detector.
  • Major social-media platforms use such GAN-trained detectors to flag fake content.

Key points to remember

  • GAN has BOTH generation (G) and detection (D) capabilities.
  • Generator: noise вҶ’ fake image.
  • Discriminator: image вҶ’ probability it is real.
  • Used in: content creation, deepfake detection, anomaly detection.

Q10 вҖ” GANs in Anomaly Detection

Asked 1 time(Nov'25 вҖ” 5 marks)
How this question appeared in past papers:

(Nov'25, 5 marks) Discuss the role of GANs in anomaly detection. How do they help identify outliers in data?

Answer

1. Definition

Anomaly detection = finding data points that are very different from the "normal" majority. Examples: fraudulent credit-card transactions, defective products on an assembly line, network intrusions, rare diseases.

2. How GANs are used for anomaly detection

  • Train the GAN ONLY on NORMAL (non-anomalous) data.
  • After training, the generator has learned the distribution of normal samples.
  • Given a new test sample x:
    • Try to find a noise vector z such that G(z) вүҲ x (i.e., reconstruct x from the latent space).
    • If x is NORMAL, this is easy вҖ” there is a z that produces a close match.
    • If x is ANOMALOUS, no good z exists вҖ” the reconstruction error is HIGH.
  • Use the reconstruction error (or discriminator score) as the anomaly score.

3. Popular GAN-based anomaly detection methods

  • AnoGAN вҖ” first GAN-based method; uses iterative search for z.
  • f-AnoGAN вҖ” faster version using a learned encoder.
  • GANomaly вҖ” uses encoder-decoder-encoder structure.
  • EGBAD вҖ” uses BiGAN (bidirectional GAN).

4. Why GANs work for anomaly detection

  • Generator learns a smooth manifold of normal data.
  • Anomalies lie OFF this manifold вҶ’ hard to reconstruct.
  • Discriminator also gives high "fake" probability for anomalies.
  • Combination of both signals = robust anomaly score.

5. Real-world applications

  • Medical imaging вҖ” detect tumours in X-rays / MRI.
  • Manufacturing вҖ” detect defective products on assembly line.
  • Cybersecurity вҖ” detect malicious network traffic.
  • Finance вҖ” fraud detection in credit-card transactions.
  • Infrastructure вҖ” detect failures in machinery (predictive maintenance).

Key points to remember

  • Train GAN only on normal data; anomalies cannot be well reconstructed.
  • Use reconstruction error / discriminator score as anomaly score.
  • Methods: AnoGAN, f-AnoGAN, GANomaly.
  • Applications: medical, manufacturing, cybersecurity, finance.
